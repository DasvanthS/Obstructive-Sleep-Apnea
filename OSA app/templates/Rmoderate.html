    Symptoms

  • AHI of 15 to 30 breathing interruptions per hour
  • Snoring and/or breathing interruptions during sleep
  • Mild to moderate decrease in oxygen levels in the blood
  • Possible daytime sleepiness and fatigue

    Treatments

  • Continuous positive airway pressure (CPAP) therapy is the most common treatment for moderate to severe OSA. It involves wearing a mask over your nose or mouth that delivers a continuous stream of air to keep your airway open while you sleep.
  • Bi-level positive airway pressure (BiPAP) therapy is similar to CPAP but delivers different pressures for inhaling and exhaling.
