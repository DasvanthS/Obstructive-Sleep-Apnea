Severe Obstructive Sleep Apnea

  • AHI of more than 30 breathing interruptions per hour
  • Loud snoring and/or frequent breathing interruptions during sleep
  • Significant decrease in oxygen levels in the blood
  • Frequent daytime sleepiness and fatigue
  • Increased risk for serious health complications such as high blood pressure, heart disease, and stroke

Treatments for Severe OSA

  • In addition to CPAP or BiPAP therapy, other treatments for severe OSA may include surgery to remove excess tissue in the throat, or to reposition the jaw or tongue.
  • Another option is to use an implanted device that stimulates the nerves controlling the tongue to keep the airway open during sleep.
